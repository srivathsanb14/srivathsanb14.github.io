About

Science and product, one pipeline

I build systems that accelerate scientific discovery and power products. Multimodal learning, GNNs, LLMs. Published in Nature Machine Intelligence and ACS JCIM. Production systems that cut costs and unlock capabilities.

I'm an M.S. graduate in AI Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University, where I worked as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Mechanical and AI Lab (MAIL), publishing AI4Science research and shipping generative AI pipelines used well beyond the lab. Before CMU, I studied Chemical Engineering at IIT Madras and worked on real-time process optimization and computer-vision pipelines in industry.

What ties the work together is a bias toward finishing: papers that get published, libraries that get released, and models that get deployed rather than left as notebooks. I care as much about clean evaluation and reproducibility as I do about the elegance of an architecture.

Research

Publications

Peer-reviewed work at the intersection of multimodal learning and molecular science.

ACS JCIM

Multi-Peptide: Multimodality Leveraged Language-Graph Learning of Peptide Properties

S. Badrinarayanan et al.  ·  arXiv:2407.03380

A multimodal model for peptide property prediction with applications in drug development and bioinformatics. Achieves state-of-the-art results using a contrastive learning framework (CLIP-style) between protein sequence transformers and AlphaFold-generated structures (GNNs).

Paper Code
Nature Machine Intelligence

Multimodal Language and Graph Learning of Adsorption Configuration in Catalysis

J. Ock, S. Badrinarayanan et al.

A generative-predictive pipeline that predicts adsorption energies without atomic spatial coordinates. A transferable model integrating LLMs with graph-assisted multimodal pretraining reduces MAE by ~10%, improving adsorption energy prediction for catalyst screening.
